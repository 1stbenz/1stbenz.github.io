## Installation Process

### Required Tools
1. 10mm Wrench (Socket or Open-end)
2. OBD-II Memory Saver (For backup power)
3. Large-to-Small Terminal Adapters (If your car uses JIS small terminals)

### Step 1: Connect Backup Power
Plug the memory saver into the OBD-II port. This ensures the car's settings (ECU data, clock, etc.) are not wiped when the main battery is disconnected.

### Step 2: Remove the Old Battery
1. **Loosen the Negative (-) Terminal first** and move it aside.
2. Loosen the Positive (+) Terminal.
3. Remove the battery hold-down bracket using your 10mm wrench.

### Step 3: Terminal Adaptation
Since the Sentra B17 uses small (JIS) terminals, but the Houny battery has large posts, I installed **copper terminal adapters**. These sleeves sit over the battery posts, allowing the original terminal clamps to fit perfectly.

### Step 4: Install the New Battery
1. Place the Houny battery into the tray.
2. Secure the hold-down bracket.
3. **Connect the Positive (+) Terminal first.**
4. Connect the Negative (-) Terminal last.

## Conclusion
Upgrading to a LiFePO4 battery like the Houny unit provides much more stable voltage and a significantly longer lifespan compared to traditional lead-acid batteries. For Sentra owners, just be mindful of the terminal size mismatch and ensure you have the correct adapters before you start.
