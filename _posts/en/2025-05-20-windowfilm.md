faq:
  - question: "What is the primary recommendation when purchasing window tint?"
    answer: "It is highly recommended to stick with major international brands if your budget allows. Smaller 'rebranded' labels often have fabricated data, unverifiable manufacturers, and potential risks of releasing carcinogens under high heat."
  - question: "Which are the prominent international window tint brands?"
    answer: "Major IWFA-certified brands include Solargard (distributed by Sun Mark in Taiwan), V-KOOL (distributed by Teapo), 3M, and Eastman brands like LLumar and SunTek."
  - question: "What is TSER, and why is it important?"
    answer: "TSER (Total Solar Energy Rejected) is the most comprehensive metric. Unlike IRR, which only measures one infrared band, TSER accounts for visible light, UV, and infrared energy across the entire solar spectrum."
  - question: "What are the pros and cons of Metallic (Reflective) films?"
    answer: "Metallic films reflect heat outward, providing long-lasting performance. However, they can block GPS, eTag, and mobile signals."
  - question: "What are the pros and cons of Ceramic (Absorptive) films?"
    answer: "Ceramic films do not block signals and are highly transparent. However, they work by absorbing heat into the glass, which can lead to 'heat re-radiation' into the cabin over time, and the performance may degrade faster than metallic films."
