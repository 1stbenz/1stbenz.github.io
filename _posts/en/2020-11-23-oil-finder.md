        <h3 style="border: none; margin-top: 0px; padding: 0px;">Oil Change Interval Planning</h3>
        <p>In Germany, engine oils are typically categorized into <span class="highlight-text">15,000 km</span> and <span class="highlight-text">25,000 km</span> drain intervals.</p>
        <p>However, the maintenance intervals recommended in Taiwan are often only 2/3 of those abroad. If an oil is rated for 15,000 km in Europe, the local dealer might suggest a 10,000 km interval. Based on this ratio, a "long-life" oil rated for 25,000 km should be changed at approximately 16,600 km in Taiwan.</p>
        <p><strong>Conclusion: Considering that Taiwan's driving conditions and climate are among the harshest globally, a 15,000 km interval is a safe and recommended middle ground.</strong></p>
        
        <hr style="border-bottom: 0px; border-top: 1px dashed #ccc; margin: 20px 0px;" />

        <h3 style="border: none; margin-top: 0px; padding: 0px;">Approvals and TBN Metrics</h3>
        <p>You may notice that while some oil manufacturers suggest a 15,000 km interval for W205 using both MB 229.51 and 229.5 approvals, it is rare to see MB 229.51/229.52 approvals for the 25,000 km ultra-long intervals.</p>
        
        <p>The main reason is the <strong>TBN (Total Base Number)</strong>, which measures the oil's ability to neutralize acids:</p>
        
        <table class="oil-table">
            <thead>
                <tr>
                    <th>Approval Standard</th>
                    <th>TBN Value</th>
                    <th>Characteristics</th>
                </tr>
            </thead>
            <tbody>
                <tr>
                    <td><strong>MB 229.5</strong></td>
                    <td>≥ 10</td>
                    <td>High TBN, strong acid neutralization, suitable for long intervals.</td>
                </tr>
                <tr>
                    <td><strong>MB 229.51 / .52</strong></td>
                    <td>≈ 6</td>
                    <td>Lower TBN, typically designed to protect emission systems (DPF).</td>
                </tr>
            </tbody>
        </table>
        
        <p>Therefore, oils intended for extended mileage usually feature a higher TBN to ensure long-term engine protection.</p>
        <p style="color: #ff7b72; font-weight: bold; margin-top: 15px;">⚠️ Important Reminder: Extended drain intervals MUST be paired with "Long-Life" filters with increased capacity. Use generic filters at your own risk.</p>
